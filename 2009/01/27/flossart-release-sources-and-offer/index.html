</br>WHAT:
</br>
</br>What does Free and Open Source software (FLOSS) provide to artists and
</br>designers - beyond just free alternatives to established tools from
</br>Photoshop to Final Cut?
</br>
</br>“FLOSS+Art” is the first book to answer this question. It shows how the
</br>value of Free Software lies in its differences and creative challenges,
</br>as opposed to out-of-the-box and off-the-shelf solutions; how it allows
</br>to work and collaborate differently with computers, and therefore enable
</br>different kinds of art and design.
</br>
</br>The Internet - whose infrastructure is based on free software and open
</br>standards - is an obvious example of such an adaptable, collaborative
</br>artistic medium. “FLOSS+Art” also covers more traditional artistic
</br>domains such as graphic and audiovisual design, and how they shift from
</br>customer-centric to community-driven work.
</br>
</br>FLOSS+Art critically reflects on the growing relationship between Free
</br>Software philosophy, open content and digital art. It provides
</br>first-hand insight into its social, political and economic myths and
</br>realities.
</br>
</br>With contributions by: Fabianne Balvedi, Florian Cramer, Sher Doruff,
</br>Nancy Mauro Flude, Olga Goriunova, Dave Griffiths, Ross Harley, Martin
</br>Howse, Shahee Ilyas, Ricardo Lafuente, Ivan Monroy Lopez, Thor
</br>Magnusson, Alex McLean, Rob Myers, Alejandra Maria Perez Nuñez, Eleonora
</br>Oreggia, oRx-qX, Julien Ottavi, Michael van Schaik, Femke Snelting,
</br>Pedro Soler, Hans Christoph Steiner, Prodromos Tsiavos, Simon Yuill.
</br>
</br>Compiled and edited by Aymeric Mansoux and Marloes de Valk.
</br>With the support of the University of Huddersfield and the Willem de
</br>Kooning Academy Rotterdam University.
